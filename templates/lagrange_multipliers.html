The condition

On the constraint curve, you are only allowed to move in tangent directions. At a constrained max or min, moving along any allowed tangent direction should not change f to first order. That means ∇f is perpendicular to the constraint tangent. Since ∇g is also perpendicular to the constraint tangent, the two gradients must be parallel.

What λ means

λ tells how much the optimal value of f changes when the constraint value c is relaxed a little.
How to read the views
3D shows z=f(x,y) as the objective surface, with the constraint lifted onto it. Top-down shows the contour map: the optimum happens where a blue f-contour just kisses the red constraint curve.
