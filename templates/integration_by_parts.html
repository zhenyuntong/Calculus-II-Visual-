LEARN  Integration by Parts — trading one hard integral for an easier one ▶
The Idea

The product rule says d(uv)/dx = u·v′ + v·u′. Rearranging and integrating gives ∫u·v′dx = uv − ∫v·u′dx. It works because the total change in the product uv can be split into the part caused by v changing and the part caused by u changing.

Formula Decoded
Original function: h(x)=u(x)v′(x)
∫u dv = [u·v]ₐᵇ − ∫v du
[u·v]ₐᵇ ← boundary term
∫v du ← remainder (hopefully easier)
LIATE rule: Log, Inv-trig, Algebraic, Trig, Exp
