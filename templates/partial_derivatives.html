What They Are

∂f/∂x = rate of change holding y fixed. ∂f/∂y = rate of change holding x fixed. They work because a tiny move can be split into an x-move plus a y-move; the partial derivatives measure those two independent first-order contributions.

The Gradient ∇f
∇f = (∂f/∂x, ∂f/∂y)
• Points in direction of steepest ascent
• ‖∇f‖ = slope of steepest direction
• Perpendicular to level curves because moving along a level curve keeps f constant
