Riemann Sum

LEARN  Riemann sums — why rectangles become the integral ▶
The idea

The original function is the curve f(x). Each method replaces a tiny curved strip of area with a simpler shape: left, right, and midpoint use rectangles; trapezoid uses a slanted top. As n grows, each strip gets thinner, so the shape error shrinks.

Why it works

The definite integral is the limit of these signed area approximations as the widest slice goes to 0.
