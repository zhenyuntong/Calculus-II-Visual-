LEARN  Leibniz Rule — differentiating under the integral sign ▶
The Three Pieces
d/dt ∫[a(t)]^[b(t)] f(x,t) dx =
  f(b(t),t)·b′(t) — upper bound sweeping
  −f(a(t),t)·a′(t) — lower bound sweeping
  +∫[a]^[b] ∂f/∂t dx — integrand changing
When ∂f/∂t = 0

If f doesn't depend on t, the blue term vanishes and you just get f(b)·b′ − f(a)·a′ — this is the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus!
